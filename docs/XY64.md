# XY64 (titled XY64)

*Part of [pages64](../README.md).*

This module turns the whole grid into a single **XY pad** — the 2D sibling of
Sliders64. Press a pad and it becomes the **target**, shown dim; the bright
**cursor** glides toward it in a straight line at the selected speed, and the
two CV outputs follow the cursor.

**Slew (scene buttons A–H):** the Sliders64 idiom — A is instant, H the
slowest (16 s across the full range); the default is C (0.5 s). The lit scene
button shows the current rate.

The cursor is **continuous**: the pads quantize only the targets, never the
glide, so a slow sweep moves smoothly through the whole 0–10 V range. At
instant speed the cursor simply jumps — and because the **TRIG** output fires
whenever the cursor *lands*, scene A turns XY64 into a trigger pad with
position CV: one trigger per press, X/Y telling you where. At slower speeds
TRIG marks the end of each glide — patch it into an envelope to articulate
arrivals.

Glides keep moving while another page is active, like Sliders64's slews.

**Outputs:** **X** (0 V left … 10 V right), **Y** (0 V bottom … 10 V top),
**TRIG** (1 ms on arrival).

In the right-click menu you can choose the **cursor** and **target** colors.
The design rationale lives in [XY64.md](design/XY64.md).
