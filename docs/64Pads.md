# 64Pads (titled 64PADS)

*Part of [pages64](../README.md).*

64Pads is the Launchpad on your screen: an interactive mirror of the grid.
Attach it directly to the **left** of Base64 (the page chain stays on the
right, as always) and it shows exactly what Base64 last sent to the device —
the 8×8 pad matrix, the top round buttons and the scene buttons, in the
hardware's red/green LED palette.

Every control is **clickable**, and a click enters Base64 through the same
path as hardware MIDI: press on mouse-down, release on mouse-up. Toggling
Grid64 buttons, cutting Mlr64 slices, drawing Life64 cells, playing Keys64 —
everything single-press works with the mouse alone. What a mouse cannot do is
*hold several pads at once*, so two-button gestures (Flin64 lengths, Mlr64
sub-loops, chords) still want the hardware.

**Page switching without hardware:** the hold-button-8 gesture becomes a
latch. Click the top round button **8** — a blue ring marks the armed
latch and the grid shows the page-select overlay — then click a pad to pick
the page; the latch releases itself. Clicking **8** again cancels.

64Pads is fully functional with no Launchpad connected (or none selected in
Base64): use it to audition the plugin before buying hardware, to build
patches on the road, or for screenshots and demos. When it is not attached
directly to the left of Base64 the display dims and clicks are ignored.

It has no jacks and no parameters — it is a window, not a processor. The
display dims when disconnected; everything else lives in Base64.
